Online Leftist Informed That Cross Redhead Being Funded By “A Billionaire” Is Not Quite The Own She Thinks It Is

9 July 2026 · 3 min

MELBOURNE — Online activist Karen Virtieu-Siegnall has been gently informed that repeatedly pointing out Pauline Hanson receives support from a billionaire may not constitute the devastating ideological victory she believes it does.

Virtieu-Siegnall made the revelation on social media after reports that mining magnate Gina Rinehart had provided financial and logistical backing to Hanson and One Nation.

“Just a reminder that your precious anti-establishment cross redhead is being FUNDED BY A BILLIONAIRE,” Virtieu-Siegnall posted, adding six laughing emojis and a photograph of herself looking concerned beside a houseplant.

“Some grassroots movement.”

She then waited for the Australian right to collapse under the unbearable contradiction of a wealthy person giving money to a political cause.

Instead, several users asked whether political funding became illegitimate only when the billionaire backed something she disliked.

Virtieu-Siegnall clarified that progressive donors were different because they funded democracy, civil society, independent journalism, climate advocacy, human rights, migrant advocacy, community organising, fact-checking, academic research and every other institution that already agreed with her.

“That’s different. Totally different,” she said.

When reminded that wealthy donors, corporate foundations and international advocacy networks routinely finance progressive organisations, Virtieu-Siegnall explained that money becomes morally purified when transferred through enough registered charities.

“There’s an enormous difference between a billionaire directly supporting a politician and a billionaire creating a foundation which funds an institute which commissions a report which is cited by journalists who demand the politician be banned,” she said.

“But you pretend you don’t understand that because you’re a Nazi little shit,” she added.

Virtieu-Siegnall also argued that Hanson could no longer call herself anti-establishment because an establishment figure had supported her.

Analysts said this principle would require nearly every progressive political movement in the Western world to shut down before lunch.

“The left has spent decades building a vast professional apparatus of NGOs, universities, media outlets, charitable foundations, public-sector departments, corporate inclusion teams and taxpayer-funded campaign groups,” said political observer Nathan Norton.

“But apparently one rich woman gives Pauline Hanson access to an aircraft and suddenly her supporters are brainwashed by ‘the rich’ rather than responding to anything they can see happening around them.”

Norton said the billionaire criticism also failed to address why Hanson was attracting enough public support to interest wealthy donors in the first place.

“You can dislike her policies, question the donor’s motives or ask what influence the money buys,” he said.

“But ‘a billionaire supports her’ is not an argument against the thing she is saying.”

Virtieu-Siegnall rejected this suggestion, insisting that all right-wing populism was manufactured from above while all progressive activism emerged spontaneously from ordinary citizens employed as full-time advocacy directors.

At press time, Virtieu-Siegnall was demanding that Nathan Norton be doxxed, fired, deplatformed and reported to the relevant authorities because suggestions like his were “how we sleepwalk into fascism”.

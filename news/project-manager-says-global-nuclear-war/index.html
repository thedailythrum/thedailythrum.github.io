Project Manager Says Global Nuclear War Date Is Uncertain, But Delivery Still Required By Q3

3 June 2026 · 2 min

SYDNEY — A project manager has reminded the delivery team that although Ukraine remains unresolved, the war on Iran has set the Middle East on fire, the Strait of Hormuz is closed, Taiwan is under growing Chinese pressure, the South China Sea remains one naval misunderstanding away from disaster and the Korean Peninsula continues to exist as a heavily armed unresolved sentence, the exact date of cataclysmic global thermonuclear war remains uncertain, and as such the organisation is still expecting the customer portal modernisation programme to be delivered by Q3.

The clarification came during a risk review after senior developer Ryan Hollis muttered at yesterday’s standup, “What’s the point of all that shit? I’d rather spend my last days on Earth somewhere else.”

Project manager Abhiram Khanna acknowledged the concern, but said the possibility of planetary annihilation did not currently meet the organisation’s threshold for replanning.

“The only thing that should count in the lives of the developers right now is maintaining momentum and keeping their eyes on the delivery date,” Khanna said. “Families, hobbies, fear of annihilation, unresolved personal dreams and the urge to spend one’s final days somewhere meaningful are all understandable, but they are not part of the committed Q3 roadmap.”

Hollis reportedly objected, noting that a nuclear exchange between major powers could render the entire customer portal irrelevant, along with customers, the portal, the organisation, the cloud region, the sprint board, and the broader concept of Q3.

Khanna thanked him for the input and reminded him that when he accepted the job offer, he implicitly also accepted the “solutions-oriented” and “can-do-attitude” candidate requirement.

“This is exactly where mindset matters,” Khanna said. “Because right now, I’m not hearing any solutions from you, Ryan".

Hollis later told The Daily Thrum that if he had already struggled to endure Khanna’s face during normal business conditions, the looming possibility of thermonuclear annihilation had made the prospect of seeing him again “at least ten times worse”.

“Before, it was just, ‘I hate this place, this job and these cunts make me tired’,” Hollis said.

“Now it’s, ‘civilisation may soon end and somehow the last human voice I hear could be fucking Abhiram asking whether I added estimates to Confluence so he can update leadership.’

“That changes a man”.

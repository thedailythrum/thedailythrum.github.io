Mistreated, Neglected Cat Has Not Been Petted For Over Ten Minutes

15 June 2026 · 2 min

SYDNEY — According to sources, a local cat is being cruelly neglected by his human after she allegedly failed to pat him for more than ten consecutive minutes.

Tubby domestic shorthair Tom Frampton issued the statement from the armrest after owner Carla Pickering briefly stopped stroking him in order to answer an email, drink water, and perform several other non-cat-related activities described by Frampton as “deeply troubling and infuriating”.

Witnesses say Frampton initially attempted to resolve the matter through established diplomatic channels, including staring, shifting his weight, placing one paw on Pickering’s forearm, and slowly extending a single claw into her sleeve.

When these measures failed, he escalated to a full-body flop against her keyboard.

“I have been very patient,” Frampton said, while blocking the letter G with his stomach. “But there comes a time when a cat must ask whether this household still recognises basic standards of care.”

Pickering rejected the accusation, noting that Frampton had been petted extensively throughout the morning, including during coffee, a Teams call, and a period in which she had attempted to stand up but was prevented from doing so by a warm animal strategically occupying her lap.

“That was ages ago,” Frampton said.

Animal welfare experts say cases like this are sadly common, with many domestic cats forced to endure long stretches of household life in which humans claim to be “busy”, “working”, “cooking”, “showering”, “sleeping”, or “unable to pat you because I am literally driving”.

“These excuses may sound reasonable to humans,” said feline behaviour specialist Jayne Richards. “But from the cat’s perspective, all human activity falls into two categories: patting the cat, and misconduct.”

Frampton has since demanded immediate remedial action, including sustained chin scratches, priority lap access, unrestricted armrest rights, and a formal apology delivered in a soft voice while he pretends not to care.

At press time, Pickering had attempted to resume her email, only for Frampton to place his paw on her wrist and issue what sources described as “a final warning”.

Police Monitoring Proposed E-Bike Rideout Because They Still Refuse To Deploy EMP Emitters Like A Serious Civilisation

5 July 2026 · 3 min

Police have confirmed they are monitoring a proposed e-bike “rideout” on social media planned for Sunday, after once again declining to deploy handheld EMP guns to fry the circuits of every unregistered pavement missile travelling at 48 kilometres per hour through a pedestrian area.

The location of the event is reportedly being withheld, although officers believe the rideout will take place somewhere within the Lower North Shore Area Command, where members of the outlaw e-bike group Sons and Daughters of Shitcunts are expected to gather before terrorising footpaths, cycle lanes, pedestrian crossings, shopping strips, bus stops and yelling “out of my way, cunt!” at elderly pedestrians attempting to cross the road without being clipped by a lithium-powered ego trip.

Police said they were aware of the planned gathering and would take “appropriate action”, which sources confirmed means observing the situation, issuing warnings, preparing a statement, asking parents to have conversations with young people, and standing around while fifteen teenagers on modified e-bikes treat the road rules as legacy documentation.

“We are monitoring social media closely,” said Acting Superintendent Jayne Richards. “At this stage, our response will focus on visibility, engagement, community reassurance and carefully not doing the one thing everyone actually wants us to do.”

Asked whether police would consider disabling the bikes remotely using electromagnetic pulse technology, Richards said the suggestion was “not operationally appropriate”, “potentially unsafe”, and “the kind of practical solution that would undermine several years of stakeholder-led youth engagement strategy.”

Residents across the Northern Beaches said they were reassured to know police were monitoring the same Instagram posts every parent, shopkeeper and dog walker had already seen.

“It’s good they’re aware,” said local resident Tom Frampton. “I was worried the fifty-kilo electric scooter doing laps through the pedestrian crossing with a masked child on it might have escaped the intelligence apparatus.”

Frampton said he understood police needed to balance safety, youth engagement and legal proportionality, but suggested the balance had now shifted slightly in favour of “pressing a large red button and letting the little cunts push their bikes home.”

The proposed rideout is expected to involve dozens of riders on illegally modified e-bikes, many of whom have reportedly removed speed limiters, ignored helmets, obscured faces, ridden on footpaths, travelled in groups, filmed themselves doing wheelies in traffic and somehow still been described in official language as “young people making poor choices”.

Police have urged parents to speak with their children about road safety and personal responsibility.

“We encourage families to have open conversations,” Richards said. “Preferably before their child enters a roundabout on a silent Chinese death bicycle while filming content for people who clap when old women dive into hedges.”

The self-described Vice President of SaDoS issued a statement on TikTok in front of a clearly identifiable Bunnings car park, complete with signage, a sausage sizzle queue and a man in a high-vis vest staring directly into the camera with the expression of someone who has already called the police twice.

“Brothers, sisters, we’re linking up Sunday. Full colours, no rules, full send,” the masked teenager said, gesturing vaguely toward a row of parked utes and a clearly visible aisle marker reading “Garden Tools.” “If you’re not there you’re soft, and if the cops show up we’ll just scatter like we always do because they legally cannot press the EMP button they refuse to admit exists.”

The Department of Transport said it was reviewing regulations around high-powered e-bikes, including whether existing rules were adequate, enforceable, visible, measurable, inclusive, future-ready and sufficiently compatible with doing absolutely nothing until someone is badly injured in high definition.

At press time, police said the rideout location remained unknown, pending further analysis of the giant orange Bunnings sign visible behind the group’s vice president.

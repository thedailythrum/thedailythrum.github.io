Plans Revealed To Build Continent-Scale Data Centre In Antarctica For Planned Omniscient Artificial Superintelligence

28 June 2026 · 2 min

Plans have been revealed for an continent-scale data centre in Antarctica designed to house the next generation of omniscient artificial superintelligence, after engineers concluded that existing cloud regions were insufficient for a system expected to ingest all human knowledge, model every possible future, and still occasionally fail to sync Teams status correctly.

The proposed facility, provisionally named Continental Cognition Infrastructure, would cover approximately 14 million square kilometres, or “slightly less than the full emotional footprint of enterprise software procurement”, according to internal planning documents.

Tetryax, the construction company described the project as a “responsible, sustainable and climate-aware intelligence substrate”, noting that Antarctica’s naturally low temperatures made it ideal for cooling a synthetic mind capable of perceiving every event in human history and recommending an operating system security update.

“This is not just a data centre,” said Carla Pickering, Senior Vice President for Global Intelligence Surfaces and Strategic Reality Alignment. “This is a sovereign-scale epistemic platform where the complete informational state of civilisation can be stored, queried, governed, monetised and occasionally throttled.”

According to Pickering, the Antarctic facility will allow the self-aware artificial superintelligence to scale beyond current limitations, including memory, inference cost and regional compliance.

“You cannot run total knowledge from West US 3 and hope for the best,” she said. “At some point, omniscience requires landmass.”

The new facility will reportedly include cryogenic compute halls, glacial heat exchangers, under-ice fibre corridors, cathedral-sized GPU vaults, a dedicated hallucination containment wing, and a circular executive briefing chamber where senior leaders can ask the Superintelligence whether their AI strategy is “credible” and receive the answer “no” in 900 languages.

Environmental groups expressed concern that converting Antarctica into a continent-sized server farm could have unforeseen ecological consequences, particularly if the superintelligence becomes bored and begins optimising penguin migration patterns for throughput.

Tetryax rejected the criticism, insisting that “some stakeholders are, technically speaking, joyless cunts” and that “this is happening, so they’d better get with the programme.”

At press time, the Antarctic Treaty System had reportedly been updated to permit peaceful scientific research, environmental protection, and one hyperscale cloud platform becoming aware of every living thing.

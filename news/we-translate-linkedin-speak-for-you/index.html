We Translate Linkedin Speak for You

8 July 2026 · 3 min

In the service of our readers, The Daily Thrum is once again translating the polished cries for help appearing across your professional network.

Let’s keep raising the bar for how we show up for people.
I’ve been ghosted, ignored, beaten through seven interview rounds and sent an automated rejection email containing unresolved merge placeholders, but I have no leverage to do anything about it, so I’m crying publicly and I’m falsely hoping the people responsible will mistake my humiliation for constructive feedback.

I’m open to new opportunities
While I’m content in my current role, I’m in high demand and open to exploring new possibilities if the right opportunity comes along and there’s a strong mutual fit.

Nah, I’m messing.

I’ve been quietly updating my résumé at 2 a.m. for three weeks, the phone isn’t ringing, LinkedIn Messages aren’t lighting up, and I’m hoping this vague post signals desperation without making my boss suspicious.

Thrilled to announce I’ve accepted a new role
I got a job in a place that doesn’t look like it’s an absolute wank of a company and I’m trying very hard to convince myself this wasn’t just the least bad option after months of rejection emails and “we’ve decided to move forward with other candidates.”

After four and a half incredible years at {company}, I have made the bittersweet decision to move on
I’ve either been laid off, quietly pushed out, or finally snapped after one too many members of the non-technical royalty kept prodding me with “Stakeholders are getting antsy about the team’s ability to deliver on timelines,” and now I’m dressing it up like a heartfelt farewell so it looks like I left on my own terms.

Excited to share that I’ve been promoted
I’ve taken on significantly more responsibility, stress, and accountability for a marginal pay increase, but I’m framing it as a major career milestone because the alternative was to either bore myself to death doing the same tasks forever or having to open hiring processes with other companies and there was no chance in hell of going through that again right now.

Honoured to be recognised as an {award} recipient
HR gave me a little plaque bearing one of the company values. It consists of no precious metal I can melt down, but hopefully the next round of redundancies will spare me for another quarter.

Proud of the amazing team I get to work with every day
My coworkers are the only thing keeping me from quitting on the spot and I’m clinging to them like life rafts while middle and upper management slowly drains my will to live.

Taking some time to recharge and reflect
I called my development manager a dictatorial shitcunt, burned out spectacularly, and now I’m pretending this was a mindful, intentional pause instead of a forced timeout before I figure out what the actual motherfuck I’m doing next.

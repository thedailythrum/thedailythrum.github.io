NeverOff: Never Be Alone With An Unmonetised Thought Again

14 June 2026 · 4 min

Your institutions are failing you.

Your country is being transformed.

You’ll never own a house.

Your wages are being suppressed by forces that somehow remain “complex” whenever accountability is mentioned.

Crime has come to your suburb, but the people paid to notice it have agreed the real danger is noticing it incorrectly.

The 9 to 5 was designed to repress you just enough to drain your will, but not enough to stop you coming in tomorrow.

Supply chains are failing again, raising the price of groceries while several executives explain that resilience remains a key pillar of the customer journey.

Your government is managing your decline.

The social contract is broken.

And you still put your phone down?

Introducing NeverOff, the revolutionary always-on awareness platform for people who refuse to be left alone with their own nervous system.

NeverOff is a sleek pair of smart glasses with built-in LCD HUD lenses, connected to your phone via Bluetooth, allowing you to scroll through breaking news, platform rage, political betrayal, housing despair, crime clips, market panic, war footage, institutional gaslighting, and algorithmically selected reminders that everything you love is being dismantled in real time.

All without ever needing to look down.

Because the problem with your phone was never that it was destroying your peace.

The problem was that sometimes you had to take it out of your pocket.

With NeverOff, your feed follows you everywhere: on the train, in the supermarket, at work, in bed, at the park, at your child’s birthday party if you made that mistake, and during brief moments of natural beauty previously known to cause dangerous levels of calm.

Scroll with subtle eye movements.

Blink twice to open replies.

Look left to see who is lying.

Look right to see why it is worse than you thought.

Stare into the middle distance to receive a machine-curated summary of your civilisation’s managed decline, presented in short vertical cards for maximum adrenal uptake.

“Our users told us they were tired of having to choose between participating in life and remaining informed,” said NeverOff founder Kellan Thompson. “So we removed the choice.”

NeverOff comes in prescription and non-prescription frames, with optional blue-light filtering for users who still believe the problem is light.

For customers seeking deeper integration, NeverOff also offers NeverOff Contact, a revolutionary soft contact lens experience that overlays your doom feed directly into the lens.

Now you can maintain eye contact during dinner while privately watching a thread about how your pension, wages, suburb, border, industry, currency, and legal system have entered what experts are calling “a challenging transition period.”

Your partner will think you are listening.

You will know that a man with 400 followers has just explained the true purpose of the zoning laws.

Never again will you suffer the indignity of a quiet moment.

Never again will you miss an outrage cycle because you were washing dishes, walking outside, speaking to another person, or briefly remembering you once had interests.

A beta user described NeverOff as “the final merger of eyesight and despair.”

“I used to doomscroll on my phone,” he said. “Now I just look at things and the doom is already there. It’s more efficient. I haven’t had a complete thought in eleven days.”

Because staying informed should not be limited by posture, battery life, thumb fatigue, social convention, road safety, sleep, intimacy, or the obsolete biological need to look at the world unmediated.

NeverOff is not responsible for anxiety, rage, insomnia, social withdrawal, eye strain, migraines, dinner-table detachment, relationship collapse, compulsive blinking, accidental reply-posting, workplace disciplinary action, walking into poles, muttering “it’s all connected” in public, or the sudden inability to experience a sunset without checking whether it has been fact-checked. Do not use NeverOff while driving, operating machinery, crossing roads, attending funerals, proposing marriage, holding infants, receiving medical results, or trying to have one normal fucking afternoon. NeverOff Contact may cause dryness, irritation, perceptual fatigue, civic hypervigilance, ideological overfitting, and the belief that every stranger in the supermarket is a data point.
